Overview

Frank Herbert - September 27, 1982

Frank Herbert was an American science fiction author who is best known for writing the 1965 novel, Dune. He was born on October 8, 1920 in Tacoma, Washington to Frank Herbert Sr. and Eileen Herbert. He had an interest in books and photography from a young age and held a variety of occupations throughout his career such as writing short stories, working as a newspaper journalist, photography, book reviews, ecological consultation, and giving lectures.Frank Herbert passed away in Madison, Wisconson on February 11, 1986 at the age of 65.

Dune

The Dune series by Frank Herbert is a groundbreaking and influential science fiction epic that explores a complex and vividly imagined universe. The first novel, "Dune," introduces readers to the desert planet of Arrakis, the only source of the valuable spice melange. The narrative follows the noble House Atreides and its young heir, Paul Atreides, as they navigate political intrigue, ecological challenges, and the mystical elements tied to the spice. Herbert's intricate world-building, political intricacies, and philosophical themes, including the concept of the Bene Gesserit sisterhood and the messianic figure Muad'Dib, contribute to the series' enduring legacy. Spanning multiple generations and delving into themes of power, religion, and environmentalism, the Dune series stands as a monumental achievement in the realm of science fiction literature.

Main books in the Dune series written by Frank Herbert:

  • Dune (1965)
  • Dune: Messiah (1969)
  • Childeren of Dune (1976)
  • God Emperor of Dune (1981)
  • Heretics of Dune (1984)
  • Chapterhouse: Dune (1985)

Awards & Recognition

In 1966 Dune won both the Nebula Award for best novel as well as the Hugo Award and has gone on to be considered one of the best science fiction novels of all time. And though none of his other books ever won the Hugo or Nebula awards, almost all of them went on to be New York Times Best Sellers. Frank Herbert was inducted into the Science Fiction Hall of Fame in 2006.
